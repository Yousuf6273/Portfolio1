  • Conceived, designed, and delivered a free-spinning wheel demonstrator adopted by the school as an active teaching resource for primary students — taking the project from brief to real-world deployment.
  • Modelled the full multi-component assembly in OnShape using parametric CAD, producing formal technical drawings with dimensioned orthographic views and tolerances in line with engineering drawing standards.
  • Applied lightweighting principles: introduced radial spoke cut-outs in the wheel to reduce rotational inertia and internal window cut-outs in the A-frame side panels to minimise material volume — while preserving structural integrity under bending and torsional loads.
  • Selected materials for a high strength-to-weight ratio and machinability; integrated a 3D-printed hub collar to ensure concentric wheel location and prevent axial shaft migration.
  • Manufactured via laser cutting, 3D printing, and woodworking; assembled a full mechanical system including rotating wheel, dowel shafts, and fastening hardware — achieving correct alignment and low-friction rotation.
  • Validated through physical load and function testing, identifying and resolving failure modes including frame flex and wheel eccentricity before the final build.
  • Formally assessed under the Edexcel GCSE framework — placing the project within an internationally recognised, rigorous assessment structure equivalent to an industry design portfolio review.
  • Followed a complete engineering design process (ideation → sketching → CAD → technical drawing → material selection → manufacture → test → iterate → deploy), with consultative design reviews mirroring industry DR workflows.

3-DOF Robot Arm

Designed a three-degree-of-freedom robotic arm from scratch in OnShape. Each joint modelled with precision mates and motion constraints enabling full kinematic simulation — documented as a complete technical assembly with exploded views and bill of materials.

Open in OnShape
  • Designed a three-degree-of-freedom robotic arm from scratch, with each joint modelled using precision mates and motion constraints enabling full kinematic simulation.
  • Calculated torque requirements at each joint and analysed structural load paths to determine appropriate cross-sections and material selection.
  • Produced a full technical assembly with exploded views, a bill of materials, and dimensioned orthographic drawings following ISO engineering drawing standards.
  • Material specified as AL 6061-T6 for its favourable strength-to-weight ratio and machinability.
  • End effector designed as a two-finger gripper to demonstrate workspace reach across all three joints.
